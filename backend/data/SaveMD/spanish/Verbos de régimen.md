# Verbos de régimen in Spanish: A Complete B1–C1 Tutorial

## 1. What are *verbos de régimen*?

In Spanish, some verbs require a specific preposition before their complement. This complement is called **complemento de régimen** or **complemento preposicional**.

The key idea:

> You must learn the verb and its preposition together as one lexical unit.

Not:

> *depender* = to depend + choose a preposition later

But:

> **depender de** = to depend on

So **depender de**, **pensar en**, **soñar con**, **consistir en**, **renunciar a**, **preocuparse por**, etc., should be memorized as complete units.

Spanish does not always use the same preposition as English. This is why advanced learners make errors like:

Incorrect: *Depende en la situación.*
Correct: **Depende de la situación.**

Incorrect: *Pienso sobre ti mucho.*
Correct: **Pienso en ti mucho.**

Incorrect: *Me di cuenta que era tarde.*
Formal/careful standard: **Me di cuenta de que era tarde.**

---

# 2. Core grammar: how these verbs work

A *verbo de régimen* may be followed by:

## A. A noun phrase

**Dependo de mi familia.**
I depend on my family.

Analysis:

* Verb: **depender**
* Required preposition: **de**
* Complemento de régimen: **de mi familia**

## B. A pronoun

**Confío en ella.**
I trust her.

Not:

Incorrect: *La confío.*
Correct: **Confío en ella.**

The complement remains prepositional.

## C. An infinitive

**Me acostumbré a vivir solo.**
I got used to living alone.

Structure:

> verb + preposition + infinitive

## D. A subordinate clause with **que**

**Insistió en que fuéramos puntuales.**
He/she insisted that we be punctual.

Structure:

> verb + preposition + que + conjugated verb

Important: the preposition often remains before **que**.

Correct:

**Me alegro de que hayas venido.**
I’m glad you came.

Incorrect in careful standard Spanish:

*Me alegro que hayas venido.*

This error is called **queísmo**: omitting a required preposition before **que**.

---

# 3. Very important distinction: not every “verb + preposition” is a *verbo de régimen*

Compare:

## A. Personal **a**

**Vi a Juan.**
I saw Juan.

Here **a Juan** is not a *complemento de régimen*. It is a **direct object with personal a**.

Similarly:

**Conozco a María.**
I know María.

This is not a prepositional verb. The **a** marks a human direct object.

## B. True *régimen*

**Me referí a Juan.**
I referred to Juan.

Here **referirse a** is a lexical unit. The verb requires **a**.

So:

* **ver a Juan** = direct object with personal **a**
* **referirse a Juan** = verb of régime: **referirse a**

---

# 4. Verbs with **a**

The preposition **a** often expresses:

* direction toward a goal
* movement toward an action
* adaptation
* opposition
* reference
* commitment
* access
* tendency

But remember: these are only memory aids. The real rule is lexical.

---

## 4.1 Direction, aspiration, goal

### **aspirar a**

to aspire to

**Aspira a convertirse en médico.**
He/she aspires to become a doctor.

Analysis:

* **aspirar a** expresses ambition or long-term goal.
* The complement may be a noun or infinitive.
* Not: *aspira convertirse*

Correct:

**Aspiro a un trabajo más estable.**
I aspire to a more stable job.

**Aspiro a mejorar mi español hasta C1.**
I aspire to improve my Spanish to C1.

---

### **tender a**

to tend to

**Los estudiantes adultos tienden a analizar demasiado las reglas.**
Adult students tend to overanalyse the rules.

Analysis:

* **tender a + infinitive** expresses a tendency.
* Common in B2/C1 academic writing.

More examples:

**Este tipo de error tiende a repetirse.**
This type of error tends to repeat itself.

**Los precios tienden a subir en temporada alta.**
Prices tend to rise in high season.

---

### **apuntar a**

to point toward / aim at

**Los datos apuntan a una mejora gradual.**
The data point to a gradual improvement.

Analysis:

* Common in journalistic and academic Spanish.
* Often means “suggest” or “indicate”.

---

## 4.2 Habit, adaptation, adjustment

### **acostumbrarse a**

to get used to

**Me acostumbré a hablar español todos los días.**
I got used to speaking Spanish every day.

Analysis:

* Pronominal verb: **acostumbrarse**
* Required preposition: **a**
* Often followed by noun or infinitive.

More examples:

**No me acostumbro al clima de esta ciudad.**
I can’t get used to this city’s climate.

**Se acostumbraron a trabajar bajo presión.**
They got used to working under pressure.

---

### **adaptarse a**

to adapt to

**Los inmigrantes tienen que adaptarse a una nueva cultura.**
Immigrants have to adapt to a new culture.

Analysis:

* **adaptarse a** means adjusting oneself to a situation, system, environment, or habit.

More examples:

**La empresa se adaptó rápidamente al mercado digital.**
The company quickly adapted to the digital market.

**Me cuesta adaptarme a horarios tan cambiantes.**
It is hard for me to adapt to such changing schedules.

---

## 4.3 Refusal, resistance, opposition

### **negarse a**

to refuse to

**Se negó a firmar el documento.**
He/she refused to sign the document.

Analysis:

* **negarse a + infinitive**
* Stronger and more formal than simply **no querer**.

More examples:

**Me niego a aceptar una explicación tan superficial.**
I refuse to accept such a superficial explanation.

**El testigo se negó a responder.**
The witness refused to answer.

---

### **oponerse a**

to oppose

**Muchos vecinos se oponen a la construcción del centro comercial.**
Many residents oppose the construction of the shopping centre.

Analysis:

* **oponerse a + noun**
* **oponerse a que + subjunctive** when followed by a clause.

Example with clause:

**Se oponen a que se cambie la ley.**
They oppose the law being changed.

---

### **renunciar a**

to give up / resign oneself from / renounce

**Renunció a su puesto después del escándalo.**
He/she resigned from his/her position after the scandal.

**No quiero renunciar a mis principios.**
I do not want to give up my principles.

Analysis:

* **renunciar a** means voluntarily abandoning something.
* Not: *renunciar de*

---

## 4.4 Commitment and obligation toward an action

### **comprometerse a**

to commit oneself to

**El gobierno se comprometió a reducir los impuestos.**
The government committed itself to reducing taxes.

Analysis:

* **comprometerse a + infinitive**
* Formal and common in news, politics, work, and agreements.

More examples:

**Me comprometo a terminar el informe antes del viernes.**
I commit to finishing the report before Friday.

---

### **atreverse a**

to dare to

**No se atrevió a decir la verdad.**
He/she did not dare to tell the truth.

Analysis:

* **atreverse a + infinitive**
* The action is psychologically difficult or risky.

More examples:

**¿Te atreves a hablar en público en español?**
Do you dare to speak publicly in Spanish?

---

### **decidirse a**

to finally decide to

**Después de pensarlo mucho, se decidió a estudiar francés.**
After thinking about it a lot, he/she finally decided to study French.

Analysis:

* **decidirse a** emphasizes the moment of overcoming hesitation.
* Compare: **decidir estudiar** = decide to study, more neutral.
* **decidirse a estudiar** = finally bring oneself to study.

---

## 4.5 Reference, access, contribution

### **referirse a**

to refer to

**El profesor se refería a los errores más frecuentes.**
The teacher was referring to the most common errors.

Analysis:

* **referirse a** is one of the most important B2/C1 verbs.
* Not: *referirse sobre*

More examples:

**Cuando digo “régimen”, me refiero a la preposición exigida por el verbo.**
When I say “régimen”, I refer to the preposition required by the verb.

---

### **acceder a**

to access / agree to

**No puedo acceder a la plataforma.**
I cannot access the platform.

**Finalmente accedió a ayudarnos.**
He/she finally agreed to help us.

Analysis:

* Two major meanings:

  * access: **acceder a un sitio / sistema**
  * agree: **acceder a hacer algo**

---

### **contribuir a**

to contribute to

**La lectura diaria contribuye a mejorar el vocabulario.**
Daily reading contributes to improving vocabulary.

Analysis:

* Common in formal argumentation.
* Often followed by abstract nouns or infinitives.

More examples:

**La falta de sueño contribuye al deterioro de la memoria.**
Lack of sleep contributes to memory decline.

---

# 5. Verbs with **en**

The preposition **en** often suggests:

* mental focus
* belief or trust
* involvement in an activity
* basis or foundation
* location within an abstract area
* insistence on a point

---

## 5.1 Mental focus

### **pensar en**

to think about

**Pienso en mis objetivos a largo plazo.**
I think about my long-term goals.

Analysis:

* **pensar en** = direct mental focus.
* Not: *pensar sobre* in ordinary emotional/mental focus.

Correct:

**Pienso mucho en mi familia.**
I think a lot about my family.

**Estoy pensando en cambiar de trabajo.**
I am thinking about changing jobs.

---

### Compare: **pensar en**, **pensar de**, **pensar que**

These are different lexical patterns.

## **pensar en algo/alguien**

mental focus

**Pienso en ti.**
I think about you.

## **pensar de algo/alguien**

to have an opinion about

**¿Qué piensas de esta idea?**
What do you think of this idea?

## **pensar que + clause**

to think/believe that

**Pienso que tienes razón.**
I think you are right.

Common learner trap:

Incorrect: *Pienso de ti todos los días.*
Correct: **Pienso en ti todos los días.**

Incorrect: *Pienso en que tienes razón.*
Usually: **Pienso que tienes razón.**

---

### **fijarse en**

to notice / pay attention to

**Fíjate en la preposición, no solo en el verbo.**
Pay attention to the preposition, not only to the verb.

Analysis:

* **fijarse en** means to consciously notice something.
* Very useful for language learning.

More examples:

**No me fijé en ese detalle.**
I didn’t notice that detail.

**Los estudiantes avanzados deben fijarse en las colocaciones.**
Advanced students should pay attention to collocations.

---

### **centrarse en / enfocarse en**

to focus on

**El curso se centra en los errores más frecuentes.**
The course focuses on the most frequent errors.

**Necesito enfocarme en mejorar mi fluidez.**
I need to focus on improving my fluency.

Analysis:

* **centrarse en** is common in Spain and formal writing.
* **enfocarse en** is widely used, especially in Latin America and modern contexts.

---

## 5.2 Belief, trust, confidence

### **creer en**

to believe in

**Creo en la importancia de la práctica deliberada.**
I believe in the importance of deliberate practice.

Analysis:

* **creer en** = believe in the existence, value, or reliability of something.
* Compare with **creer que**.

**Creo que este método funciona.**
I think this method works.

**Creo en este método.**
I believe in this method.

---

### **confiar en**

to trust / rely on

**Confío en tu criterio.**
I trust your judgment.

**Confío en que todo saldrá bien.**
I trust that everything will go well.

Analysis:

* With noun/pronoun: **confiar en alguien/algo**
* With clause: **confiar en que + indicative/subjunctive depending on nuance**, but often indicative when expressing confidence.

Common error:

Incorrect: *Confío que todo saldrá bien.*
Careful standard: **Confío en que todo saldrá bien.**

---

## 5.3 Structure, essence, and foundation

### **consistir en**

to consist of / consist in

**El problema consiste en que no memorizas el verbo con su preposición.**
The problem consists in the fact that you do not memorize the verb with its preposition.

Analysis:

* Extremely important B2/C1 verb.
* Often used to define the essence of a problem.

More examples:

**La tarea consiste en escribir un resumen.**
The task consists of writing a summary.

**La diferencia consiste en el grado de formalidad.**
The difference consists in the degree of formality.

---

### **basarse en**

to be based on

**La explicación se basa en ejemplos reales.**
The explanation is based on real examples.

Analysis:

* Very common in academic Spanish.
* Not: *basarse sobre* in standard usage.

More examples:

**Su argumento se basa en una falsa suposición.**
His/her argument is based on a false assumption.

---

## 5.4 Participation, influence, specialization

### **participar en**

to participate in

**Participé en una reunión sobre educación.**
I participated in a meeting about education.

Analysis:

* Not: *participar a*
* The event/activity is the domain in which you participate.

---

### **influir en**

to influence

**La lengua materna influye en los errores que cometemos.**
The mother tongue influences the mistakes we make.

Analysis:

* **influir en** is essential for formal argumentation.
* Compare with **afectar a**.

**La falta de práctica afecta a la fluidez.**
Lack of practice affects fluency.

**La falta de práctica influye en la fluidez.**
Lack of practice influences fluency.

---

### **especializarse en**

to specialize in

**Se especializó en lingüística aplicada.**
He/she specialized in applied linguistics.

Analysis:

* Field/domain = **en**
* Not: *especializarse sobre*

---

## 5.5 Insistence and persistence

### **insistir en**

to insist on

**Insistió en repetir el ejercicio.**
He/she insisted on repeating the exercise.

**Insistió en que repitiéramos el ejercicio.**
He/she insisted that we repeat the exercise.

Analysis:

* **insistir en + infinitive**
* **insistir en que + subjunctive** when expressing pressure, request, or insistence.

Common error:

Incorrect: *Insistió que viniéramos.*
Correct: **Insistió en que viniéramos.**

---

### **empeñarse en**

to insist stubbornly on / be determined to

**Se empeñó en hacerlo todo solo.**
He/she insisted on doing everything alone.

Analysis:

* Often implies stubbornness or strong determination.
* More expressive than **insistir en**.

---

# 6. Verbs with **de**

The preposition **de** often suggests:

* origin
* source
* separation
* dependency
* memory
* awareness
* lack
* responsibility
* emotional reaction
* topic

But again: learn each verb as a unit.

---

## 6.1 Dependency and source

### **depender de**

to depend on

**El éxito depende de la constancia.**
Success depends on consistency.

Analysis:

* One of the most common learner errors.
* English says “depend on”; Spanish says **depender de**.

More examples:

**Depende de cuánto tiempo tengas.**
It depends on how much time you have.

**No quiero depender de la suerte.**
I don’t want to depend on luck.

Common error:

Incorrect: *Depende en tu nivel.*
Correct: **Depende de tu nivel.**

---

### **provenir de**

to come from / originate from

**Muchos errores provienen de traducciones literales.**
Many errors come from literal translations.

Analysis:

* Common in analytical writing.
* Source/origin = **de**.

---

### **derivarse de**

to derive from

**Esta confusión se deriva de una falsa equivalencia con el inglés.**
This confusion derives from a false equivalence with English.

Analysis:

* Formal C1 verb.
* Useful for explaining causes.

---

## 6.2 Memory and awareness

### **acordarse de**

to remember

**Me acuerdo de aquella conversación.**
I remember that conversation.

**Me acuerdo de que me lo dijiste.**
I remember that you told me.

Analysis:

* Pronominal: **acordarse**
* Required preposition: **de**
* With clause: **de que**

Compare:

**Recuerdo aquella conversación.**
I remember that conversation.

No preposition after **recordar** when it is transitive.

Common contrast:

**Me acuerdo de tu nombre.**
I remember your name.

**Recuerdo tu nombre.**
I remember your name.

Both are correct, but the structures differ.

---

### **olvidarse de**

to forget

**Me olvidé de llamarte.**
I forgot to call you.

Analysis:

* Pronominal version uses **de**.
* Compare with transitive **olvidar**.

**Olvidé llamarte.**
I forgot to call you.

**Me olvidé de llamarte.**
I forgot to call you.

Both are acceptable, but the grammar is different.

---

### **darse cuenta de**

to realize

**Me di cuenta de que estaba equivocado.**
I realized that I was wrong.

Analysis:

* Fixed structure: **darse cuenta de**
* Before a clause: **de que**

Common error:

Informal/common but avoided in careful standard:

*Me di cuenta que estaba equivocado.*

Careful standard:

**Me di cuenta de que estaba equivocado.**

---

### **enterarse de**

to find out about

**Me enteré de la noticia por casualidad.**
I found out about the news by chance.

**Me enteré de que habían cancelado la clase.**
I found out that they had cancelled the class.

Analysis:

* **enterarse de** = receive information, discover something.
* With clause: **de que**

---

## 6.3 Repentance, complaint, emotion

### **arrepentirse de**

to regret / repent of

**Se arrepintió de haber mentido.**
He/she regretted having lied.

Analysis:

* Pronominal verb.
* Usually followed by noun or infinitive.
* Very common in narratives and moral reflection.

More examples:

**No me arrepiento de mi decisión.**
I do not regret my decision.

---

### **quejarse de**

to complain about

**Se queja de todo.**
He/she complains about everything.

**Los vecinos se quejaron del ruido.**
The neighbours complained about the noise.

Analysis:

* Not: *quejarse sobre*
* The object of complaint uses **de**.

---

### **alegrarse de**

to be glad about

**Me alegro de verte.**
I’m glad to see you.

**Me alegro de que hayas aprobado el examen.**
I’m glad that you passed the exam.

Analysis:

* Emotion + fact.
* With clause, Spanish often uses subjunctive after emotional reaction:

  * **Me alegro de que hayas aprobado.**

Compare:

**Me alegro por ti.**
I’m happy for you.

**Me alegro de tu éxito.**
I’m glad about your success.

---

### **avergonzarse de**

to be ashamed of

**Se avergüenza de sus errores.**
He/she is ashamed of his/her mistakes.

**No deberías avergonzarte de pedir ayuda.**
You shouldn’t be ashamed of asking for help.

---

## 6.4 Lack, possession, use

### **carecer de**

to lack

**El texto carece de coherencia.**
The text lacks coherence.

Analysis:

* Formal and common at B2/C1.
* Similar to **no tener**, but more formal.

More examples:

**Su argumento carece de fundamento.**
His/her argument lacks foundation.

---

### **disponer de**

to have available / have at one’s disposal

**Disponemos de tres horas para terminar el proyecto.**
We have three hours available to finish the project.

Analysis:

* Formal alternative to **tener**.
* Common in work, administration, academic contexts.

---

### **prescindir de**

to do without / dispense with

**Podemos prescindir de esa parte.**
We can do without that part.

Analysis:

* C1-level useful verb.
* Means something is not necessary.

---

### **abusar de**

to abuse / overuse / take advantage of

**No conviene abusar de los conectores formales.**
It is not advisable to overuse formal connectors.

Analysis:

* Can mean abusing a person, privilege, substance, or technique.
* In language learning, **abusar de** often means “overuse”.

---

## 6.5 Topic, responsibility, parts

### **tratar de**

to try to / deal with / be about

This verb is tricky because it has multiple patterns.

## **tratar de + infinitive**

to try to

**Traté de explicarlo con claridad.**
I tried to explain it clearly.

## **tratar de + noun**

to be about / deal with

**El libro trata de la memoria y el aprendizaje.**
The book is about memory and learning.

## **tratar sobre + noun**

to be about, more explicitly topic-focused

**El artículo trata sobre la inteligencia artificial.**
The article is about artificial intelligence.

## **tratar con alguien**

to deal with someone

**Es difícil tratar con personas arrogantes.**
It is difficult to deal with arrogant people.

---

### **ocuparse de**

to take care of / deal with

**Yo me ocupo de la introducción.**
I’ll take care of the introduction.

Analysis:

* Means responsibility for a task.
* Similar to **encargarse de**.

---

### **encargarse de**

to be in charge of

**Ella se encarga de organizar las reuniones.**
She is in charge of organizing the meetings.

Analysis:

* More responsibility-focused than **ocuparse de**.

---

### **constar de**

to consist of / be made up of

**El examen consta de cuatro partes.**
The exam consists of four parts.

Analysis:

* Formal and very useful.
* Similar to **consistir en**, but **constar de** emphasizes components.

Compare:

**El problema consiste en la falta de práctica.**
The problem consists in the lack of practice.

**El curso consta de diez unidades.**
The course consists of ten units.

---

* company

* interaction
* instrument
* agreement
* conformity
* connection

to count on / rely on / have

You can count on me.

* **contar con alguien** = rely on someone.
* **contar con algo** = have something available.

I dream of speaking Spanish naturally.

* For literal dream content, **soñar que + clause** is also possible.

**Soñé que vivía en España.**

I dream of living in Spain.

to marry

**Me reuní con mi profesor para revisar el ensayo.**
I met with my teacher to review the essay.

**Hay que cumplir con las normas.**

**Cumplió con su deber.**
He/she fulfilled his/her duty.

---

### **conformarse con**

to settle for / be satisfied with

**No deberías conformarte con respuestas superficiales.**

* Important B2/C1 expression.

### **comparar con**

to compare with

**No conviene comparar tu progreso con el de un nativo.**

Analysis:

* **comparar A con B**

I came across a problem.

* motive
* concern
* search
* preference
* exchange
* struggle
* route/process
* representation
* support

## 8.1 Concern and interest

### **preocuparse por**

to worry about / care about

**Me preocupo por mi futuro.**
I worry about my future.

Analysis:

* Emotional concern = **por**
* Common with people, problems, future, health.

More examples:

**Los padres se preocupan por la educación de sus hijos.**
Parents worry about their children’s education.

**No te preocupes por eso.**
Don’t worry about that.

to take interest in

**Me intereso por la lingüística española.**
I am interested in Spanish linguistics.

Analysis:

* **interesarse por** emphasizes active interest.

  * **estar interesado en** = to be interested in
  * **interesarse por** = to take an interest in

## 8.2 Search, inquiry, asking

### **preguntar por**

to ask about / ask for someone

**Preguntó por ti.**
He/she asked about you.

**Llamé para preguntar por el precio.**
I called to ask about the price.

Analysis:

* **preguntar por alguien/algo**
* But **preguntar algo a alguien** is different.

Compare:

**Le pregunté la hora.**
I asked him/her the time.

**Pregunté por tu hermano.**
I asked about your brother.

## 8.3 Preference, choice, support

### **optar por**

**Opté por una solución más sencilla.**
I opted for a simpler solution.

Many students opt for memorizing lists, but that is not enough.

---

### **decidirse por**

to choose / settle on

**Al final me decidí por el curso intensivo.**
In the end I chose the intensive course.

Analysis:

* **decidirse por + noun**
* Compare:

  * **decidirse a + infinitive** = finally decide to do something
  * **decidirse por + noun** = choose one option

Examples:

**Me decidí a estudiar francés.**

**Me decidí por el francés.**
I chose French.

---

### **apostar por**

to bet on / support

**La empresa apuesta por la innovación.**

Analysis:

* Very common in journalism and business Spanish.

to be useful for / serve to

to be suitable for

This example is useful/suitable for explaining several errors at once.

* Informal or semi-formal.

## 9.2 Preparation and training

### **prepararse para**

to prepare for

**Me preparo para el examen DELE B2.**
I am preparing for the DELE B2 exam.

I am preparing to speak more fluently.

Analysis:

* Goal/event = **para**

### **aprovechar para**

to take the opportunity to

**Aproveché para repasar los verbos de régimen.**
I took the opportunity to review prepositional verbs.

to arrange to meet in order to

**Quedamos para estudiar juntos.**
We arranged to meet to study together.

Analysis:

* Do not confuse with **quedar en**.

# 10. Verbs with **sobre**

The preposition **sobre** usually introduces:

* topic
* subject matter
* area of discussion
* reflection
* opinion
* information

It often overlaps with **de**, but **sobre** is more explicitly “about/on the topic of”.

---

## 10.1 Speaking, writing, discussing

### **hablar sobre / hablar de**

to speak about

Both are correct, but the nuance differs slightly.

**Hablamos de tu progreso.**
We talked about your progress.

**Hablamos sobre los principales errores de los estudiantes avanzados.**
We talked about the main errors of advanced students.

Analysis:

* **de** can feel more general or natural in conversation.
* **sobre** can feel more topic-focused or formal.

---

### **tratar sobre**

to be about

**El artículo trata sobre la adquisición de segundas lenguas.**
The article is about second-language acquisition.

Analysis:

* Topic of an article/book/speech.
* More explicitly topical than **tratar de**.

---

### **versar sobre**

to be about / deal with

**La conferencia versa sobre los cambios lingüísticos actuales.**
The lecture deals with current linguistic changes.

Analysis:

* Formal C1 verb.
* Common in academic or official style.

---

## 10.2 Reflection, opinion, decision

### **reflexionar sobre**

to reflect on

**Conviene reflexionar sobre por qué cometemos ciertos errores.**
It is advisable to reflect on why we make certain mistakes.

Analysis:

* Cognitive reflection = **sobre**
* More formal than **pensar en**

Compare:

**Pienso en mis errores.**
I think about my mistakes.

**Reflexiono sobre mis errores.**
I reflect on my mistakes.

---

### **opinar sobre**

to give an opinion about

**No puedo opinar sobre un tema que no conozco.**
I cannot give an opinion about a topic I do not know.

Analysis:

* Common in essays, speaking exams, debates.

---

### **decidir sobre**

to decide on/about

**El comité decidirá sobre la propuesta mañana.**
The committee will decide on the proposal tomorrow.

Analysis:

* Formal/institutional.
* Compare:

  * **decidir hacer algo** = decide to do something
  * **decidir sobre algo** = make a decision regarding a matter

---

# 13. High-value contrast pairs

These are the pairs that often separate B1/B2 learners from C1 learners.

---

## 13.1 **pensar en / pensar de / pensar que**

**Pienso en mi futuro.**
I think about my future.

**¿Qué piensas de esta propuesta?**
What do you think of this proposal?

**Pienso que deberíamos esperar.**
I think we should wait.

Trap:

English “think about” may be **pensar en**, but English “think of” as “have an opinion of” is **pensar de**.

---

## 13.2 **hablar de / hablar sobre / hablar con**

**Hablé de mis planes.**
I talked about my plans.

**Hablé sobre la economía global.**
I spoke about the global economy.

**Hablé con mi jefe.**
I spoke with my boss.

Trap:

* Topic: **de/sobre**
* Person: **con**

---

## 13.3 **tratar de / tratar sobre / tratar con**

**Traté de resolver el problema.**
I tried to solve the problem.

**El libro trata de/sobre la memoria.**
The book is about memory.

**Es difícil tratar con clientes impacientes.**
It is difficult to deal with impatient clients.

Trap:

Same verb, different preposition, different meaning.

---

## 13.4 **acabar de / acabar con / acabar por / acabar en**

**Acabo de llegar.**
I have just arrived.

**Quiero acabar con este hábito.**
I want to put an end to this habit.

**Acabó por aceptar la oferta.**
He/she ended up accepting the offer.

**La discusión acabó en una pelea.**
The discussion ended in a fight.

Trap:

Do not memorize **acabar** alone. Memorize the whole pattern.

---

## 13.5 **decidirse a / decidirse por**

**Se decidió a hablar.**
He/she finally decided to speak.

**Se decidió por la opción más barata.**
He/she chose the cheapest option.

Trap:

* **a + infinitive** = finally take action
* **por + noun** = choose among options

---

## 13.6 **alegrarse de / alegrarse por**

**Me alegro de que hayas venido.**
I’m glad you came.

**Me alegro por ti.**
I’m happy for you.

Trap:

* **de** = the fact/event that causes joy
* **por** = the person/situation on whose behalf you feel joy

---

## 13.7 **recordar algo / acordarse de algo**

**Recuerdo tu nombre.**
I remember your name.

**Me acuerdo de tu nombre.**
I remember your name.

Trap:

* **recordar** usually takes a direct object.
* **acordarse de** requires **de**.

---

## 13.8 **olvidar algo / olvidarse de algo**

**Olvidé las llaves.**
I forgot the keys.

**Me olvidé de las llaves.**
I forgot the keys.

Trap:

Both are possible, but the structure changes.

---

## 13.9 **luchar por / luchar contra**

**Luchan por la libertad.**
They fight for freedom.

**Luchan contra la opresión.**
They fight against oppression.

Trap:

* Desired goal = **por**
* Enemy/problem = **contra**

---

## 13.10 **consistir en / constar de**

**El problema consiste en la falta de práctica.**
The problem consists in the lack of practice.

**El curso consta de diez lecciones.**
The course consists of ten lessons.

Trap:

* Essence/problem/definition = **consistir en**
* Components/parts = **constar de**

---

# 14. Common error patterns and cognitive traps

## Error 1: Translating the English preposition directly

Incorrect:

*Depende en tu situación.*
Because English says “depend on”.

Correct:

**Depende de tu situación.**

Other examples:

| English        | Wrong literal Spanish |         Correct Spanish |
| -------------- | --------------------: | ----------------------: |
| depend on      |         *depender en* |         **depender de** |
| think about    |        *pensar sobre* |           **pensar en** |
| dream of       |            *soñar de* |           **soñar con** |
| marry someone  |   *casarse a alguien* | **casarse con alguien** |
| insist on      |      *insistir sobre* |         **insistir en** |
| complain about |      *quejarse sobre* |         **quejarse de** |

---

## Error 2: Dropping the preposition before **que**

This is called **queísmo**.

Incorrect:

*Me di cuenta que era tarde.*
Correct:

**Me di cuenta de que era tarde.**

Incorrect:

*Insistió que fuéramos.*
Correct:

**Insistió en que fuéramos.**

Incorrect:

*Confío que lo hará bien.*
Careful standard:

**Confío en que lo hará bien.**

Pattern:

> verb + required preposition + que + conjugated verb

Examples:

**Me acuerdo de que lo mencionaste.**
I remember that you mentioned it.

**Se quejó de que nadie lo escuchaba.**
He complained that nobody listened to him.

**Insistió en que cambiáramos el plan.**
He insisted that we change the plan.

---

## Error 3: Adding **de** where it does not belong

This is called **dequeísmo**.

Incorrect:

*Creo de que tienes razón.*
Correct:

**Creo que tienes razón.**

Incorrect:

*Pienso de que es mejor esperar.*
Correct:

**Pienso que es mejor esperar.**

Incorrect:

*Espero de que vengas.*
Correct:

**Espero que vengas.**

Rule:

Use **de que** only when the verb or expression requires **de**.

Correct:

**Me alegro de que estés aquí.**
Because the structure is **alegrarse de**.

Correct:

**Me di cuenta de que era tarde.**
Because the structure is **darse cuenta de**.

But:

**Creo que...**
**Pienso que...**
**Espero que...**
**Quiero que...**

No **de**.

---

## Error 4: Forgetting that pronominal verbs often require a preposition

Many Spanish verbs change structure when they become pronominal.

Compare:

**Recordé la fecha.**
I remembered the date.

**Me acordé de la fecha.**
I remembered the date.

**Olvidé el libro.**
I forgot the book.

**Me olvidé del libro.**
I forgot the book.

**Encargué una pizza.**
I ordered a pizza.

**Me encargué de la cena.**
I took care of dinner.

Trap:

The pronoun **me/te/se/nos/os** does not replace the preposition. You still need the required preposition.

---

## Error 5: Confusing topic, person, and goal

With verbs like **hablar**, **tratar**, **preguntar**, the preposition depends on the role of the complement.

**Hablé con Ana.**
Person/interlocutor.

**Hablé de Ana.**
Topic.

**Pregunté por Ana.**
I asked about Ana / asked whether Ana was there.

**Le pregunté a Ana.**
I asked Ana.

**Traté de explicarlo.**
I tried to explain it.

**Traté con Ana.**
I dealt with Ana.

**El texto trata sobre Ana.**
The text is about Ana.

---

## Error 6: Using a preposition because it “sounds logical”

Advanced learners often reason semantically:

> “This is a topic, so I should use *sobre*.”

But Spanish often uses a fixed lexical pattern.

For example:

**quejarse de**
not usually *quejarse sobre*

**pensar en**
not usually *pensar sobre* for ordinary mental focus

**consistir en**
not *consistir de*

**depender de**
not *depender en*

The semantic logic helps, but the lexical unit decides.

---

## Error 7: Ignoring meaning changes caused by preposition changes

Spanish prepositions are not decoration. They can completely change the verb’s meaning.

| Verb pattern              |                            Meaning |
| ------------------------- | ---------------------------------: |
| **acabar de hacer algo**  |        to have just done something |
| **acabar con algo**       |         to put an end to something |
| **acabar por hacer algo** |          to end up doing something |
| **acabar en algo**        |                to end in something |
| **pensar en algo**        |           to think about something |
| **pensar de algo**        | to have an opinion about something |
| **tratar de hacer algo**  |             to try to do something |
| **tratar de/sobre algo**  |              to be about something |
| **tratar con alguien**    |               to deal with someone |
| **contar con alguien**    |                to count on someone |
| **contar algo**           |            to tell/count something |

---

# 15. Memory strategies

## Strategy 1: Memorize the question form

For each verb, memorize the natural question.

| Verb              |       Question form |
| ----------------- | ------------------: |
| **depender de**   |    ¿De qué depende? |
| **pensar en**     |    ¿En qué piensas? |
| **soñar con**     |    ¿Con qué sueñas? |
| **aspirar a**     |     ¿A qué aspiras? |
| **confiar en**    |  ¿En quién confías? |
| **quejarse de**   |  ¿De qué te quejas? |
| **luchar por**    |    ¿Por qué luchas? |
| **luchar contra** | ¿Contra qué luchas? |
| **servir para**   |    ¿Para qué sirve? |
| **tratar sobre**  |   ¿Sobre qué trata? |

This is powerful because Spanish speakers naturally retrieve the preposition through the question.

---

## Strategy 2: Memorize with a personal sentence

Do not memorize:

> depender de = depend on

Memorize:

**Mi progreso depende de la práctica diaria.**

Do not memorize:

> confiar en = trust

Memorize:

**Confío en mi capacidad de mejorar.**

Do not memorize:

> soñar con = dream of

Memorize:

**Sueño con hablar español con naturalidad.**

Personal sentences are stickier than abstract translations.

---

## Strategy 3: Create contrast cards

For confusing verbs, memorize pairs.

Example card:

**recordar algo**
**acordarse de algo**

Example:

**Recuerdo tu nombre.**
**Me acuerdo de tu nombre.**

Another card:

**decidirse a + infinitive**
**decidirse por + noun**

Example:

**Me decidí a estudiar francés.**
**Me decidí por el francés.**

---

## Strategy 4: Learn verb families

Many pronominal emotional/cognitive verbs use **de**:

* **acordarse de**
* **olvidarse de**
* **arrepentirse de**
* **alegrarse de**
* **avergonzarse de**
* **quejarse de**
* **burlarse de**
* **enterarse de**
* **darse cuenta de**

Many focus/domain verbs use **en**:

* **pensar en**
* **confiar en**
* **creer en**
* **fijarse en**
* **centrarse en**
* **basarse en**
* **consistir en**
* **participar en**
* **insistir en**
* **especializarse en**

Many relationship/contact verbs use **con**:

* **hablar con**
* **reunirse con**
* **casarse con**
* **colaborar con**
* **contar con**
* **soñar con**
* **identificarse con**
* **comparar con**
* **encontrarse con**

Many concern/choice/struggle verbs use **por**:

* **preocuparse por**
* **preguntar por**
* **interesarse por**
* **optar por**
* **decidirse por**
* **luchar por**
* **apostar por**
* **votar por**
* **velar por**
* **abogar por**

---

## Strategy 5: Use the “pronoun test”

Replace the complement with a pronoun and see whether the preposition survives.

**Dependo de mi familia.**
→ **Dependo de ella.**

**Pienso en el problema.**
→ **Pienso en ello.**

**Confío en Ana.**
→ **Confío en ella.**

**Me quejo del ruido.**
→ **Me quejo de eso.**

If the preposition remains, you are probably dealing with a true prepositional complement.

# 16. Final synthesis framework

When you meet a Spanish verb, ask these questions.

## Step 1: Is the verb transitive or prepositional?

Can it take a direct object?

**Recuerdo tu nombre.**
Direct object. No preposition.

Or does it require a fixed preposition?

**Me acuerdo de tu nombre.**
Prepositional complement.

---

## Step 2: Learn the verb as a chunk

Do not learn:

**depender**
**consistir**
**soñar**
**confiar**

Learn:

**depender de**
**consistir en**
**soñar con**
**confiar en**

---

## Step 3: Identify the complement type

### Noun:

**Depende de la situación.**

### Pronoun:

**Depende de eso.**

### Infinitive:

**Trató de explicarlo.**

### Clause:

**Me di cuenta de que era tarde.**

---

## Step 4: Preserve the preposition before **que**

If the verb requires a preposition, keep it before **que**.

**Insistió en que fuéramos.**

**Me alegro de que estés aquí.**

**Confío en que todo saldrá bien.**

---

## Step 5: Beware of English interference

English “on” does not always mean Spanish **en**.

* depend on = **depender de**
* insist on = **insistir en**
* rely on = **contar con / confiar en**
* dream of = **soñar con**
* complain about = **quejarse de**
* ask about = **preguntar por**

---

## Step 6: Check whether changing the preposition changes the meaning

Some verbs have multiple fixed patterns:

**tratar de** ≠ **tratar sobre** ≠ **tratar con**

**pensar en** ≠ **pensar de** ≠ **pensar que**

**acabar de** ≠ **acabar con** ≠ **acabar por** ≠ **acabar en**

**decidirse a** ≠ **decidirse por**

At B2/C1, you must not only know the preposition; you must know the meaning created by the whole structure.

---

# 18. The deepest rule

At beginner level, you learn:

> verb + meaning

At intermediate level, you learn:

> verb + preposition

At advanced level, you learn:

> verb + preposition + meaning + syntactic pattern + register

For example, a C1 learner should not merely know:

**tratar = to treat/try/deal**

A C1 learner should know:

* **tratar de + infinitive** = try to do something
* **tratar de/sobre + topic** = be about
* **tratar con + person** = deal with someone
* **tratar a alguien de una manera** = treat someone in a certain way

That is the correct mental model for *verbos de régimen*: not isolated verbs, but structured lexical units.
